# Product

## Register

dual

## Platform

web

## Users

**Primary — Visitors**: Wildlife enthusiasts, students, families, and casual browsers exploring animal facts and zoo content. They come to learn about animals, browse species, and discover conservation stories. Their context: browsing on mobile or desktop, seeking engaging educational content.

**Secondary — Staff/Admins**: Zoo managers and content administrators who maintain animal records, manage user accounts, and monitor platform activity. Their context: desktop-based workflow, need efficient CRUD operations and clear data views.

## Product Purpose

Wam Zoo is a dual-purpose wildlife platform that serves as both a public-facing educational showcase and an internal zoo management tool. It exists to make animal data accessible, engaging, and scientifically accurate — while giving staff the tools to manage content efficiently. Success looks like: visitors spending time exploring animal profiles, staff completing management tasks without friction, and the platform feeling trustworthy and authoritative.

## Positioning

A data-rich, authentic wildlife experience that respects both the animal and the audience — no cartoonish shortcuts, no corporate sterility.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA**: Browse Animals (explore the collection)
- **Secondary CTA**: Learn More (discover educational content)
- **The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds**: "Real animals. Real data. Real conservation."
- **Belief ladder**: 
  1. This looks professional and trustworthy
  2. The animal data is accurate and detailed
  3. I'm learning something I didn't know
  4. I want to explore more animals
  5. I'll come back for new content
- **Proof on hand**: Scientific data from API Ninjas (taxonomy, conservation status, habitat facts), Firebase-powered user accounts for personalized experience

## Brand Personality

**Wild. Smart. Real.**

- Wild: Nature-first, not corporate. The interface feels alive, not sterile.
- Smart: Educational, data-rich. Every animal profile teaches something.
- Real: Authentic, not cartoonish. Real photos, real facts, real conservation.

Voice: Authoritative but approachable. Like a knowledgeable zookeeper, not a textbook.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Bootstrap template**: Cookie-cutter cards, predictable spacing, forgettable design
- **Childish/cartoonish**: No clip art, no "fun zoo" vibe, no exaggerated playfulness
- **Overly corporate**: Not a bank or SaaS dashboard. No cold blue gradients.
- **AI slop**: No gradient text, no glassmorphism everywhere, no identical card grids, no numbered eyebrows on every section
- **Stock photo overload**: Real animal photography, not staged zoo shots

## Design Principles

1. **Data as Design**: Scientific accuracy is a feature, not a footnote. Every animal profile should feel like a museum exhibit — rich, detailed, trustworthy.

2. **Respect the Animal**: No cartoonish shortcuts. Real photos, real facts, real habitat data. The animal is the star, not the UI.

3. **Earned Complexity**: Start simple, reveal depth. A casual browser sees beauty; a curious learner finds taxonomy, conservation status, population data.

4. **Dual-Audience Harmony**: Visitors see a polished showcase; staff see an efficient tool. Same system, different lenses. Neither audience should feel like an afterthought.

5. **Conservation Without Preaching**: Let the data speak. Population numbers, threats, and habitat facts tell the story better than slogans.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance (contrast ratios, focus states, keyboard navigation)
- Screen reader support with semantic HTML and ARIA labels
- Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Skip-to-content link for keyboard users
- Focus visible rings on all interactive elements
- Form error announcements for screen readers
- Meaningful alt text for all animal images
